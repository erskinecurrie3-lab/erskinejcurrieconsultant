Worship Team Audition Checklist

How to Evaluate and Onboard New Worship Team Members

Provided by Erskine J Currie Ministries

Part 1: Pre-Audition Screening

  • Application received — Name, contact info, musical background, church involvement
  • Church membership verified — Active member for at least 3 months
  • Small group participation confirmed — Connected in community
  • Pastor/leader reference obtained — Character reference from a ministry leader
  • Worship team covenant reviewed — Candidate has read and agrees to expectations
  • Audition date scheduled — Confirmed time, location, and what to prepare

Part 3: Spiritual Assessment Interview

Questions to Ask

  • Share your testimony — How did you come to faith?
  • What does worship mean to you? — Looking for depth beyond "singing songs"
  • Describe your personal worship life — Do they worship outside of Sunday?
  • How do you handle conflict on a team? — Looking for maturity and humility
  • What is your availability? — Can they commit to rehearsals and services?
  • Are you willing to serve in a support role first? — Testing servant-heartedness

Part 4: Decision & Onboarding

  • Audition reviewed by worship leader and pastor
  • Decision communicated to candidate — Whether accepted, waitlisted, or redirected
  • If accepted: Sign worship team covenant
  • Assign a mentor/buddy on the team
  • Add to team communication channels — GroupMe, Planning Center, email list
  • Schedule first rehearsal attendance — As an observer first
  • Provide song library and resources — Charts, recordings, tutorials
  • Schedule 30-day check-in — How are they adjusting? Any concerns?
  • Schedule 90-day evaluation — Formal review of fit and progress
