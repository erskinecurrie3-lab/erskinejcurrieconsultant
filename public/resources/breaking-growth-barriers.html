5 Steps to Breaking Growth Barriers

Practical Strategies for Churches Stuck at Attendance Plateaus

By Erskine J Currie

Introduction

Every church hits growth barriers. Whether you're stuck at 50, 200, or 500, the principles for breaking through are remarkably similar. This guide identifies the five most common barriers and provides actionable strategies to overcome each one.

1 Evaluate Your Guest Experience

Growth begins with retention. If guests aren't returning after their first visit, no amount of outreach will help. Conduct an honest audit of every touchpoint:

  • Parking & Signage: Can a first-time visitor find their way without asking?
  • Greeting: Is there a warm but not overwhelming welcome?
  • Children's Ministry: Do parents feel confident leaving their kids?
  • Service Flow: Is it accessible to someone with no church background?
  • Follow-Up: Do guests hear from you within 24 hours?
Action: Invite 3 unchurched friends to visit your church and give brutally honest feedback. Their perspective is invaluable.

2 Develop Leaders, Not Just Volunteers

Churches plateau when the pastor becomes the bottleneck. The solution isn't working harder — it's developing leaders who can carry the vision.

  • Identify your top 10% of volunteers and invest deeply in them
  • Create a clear leadership pipeline with defined levels
  • Delegate authority, not just tasks
  • Meet regularly with emerging leaders for mentoring
  • Celebrate and publicly affirm leadership development

3 Multiply Your Groups and Services

Growth often requires multiplication. When your small groups are full, start new ones. When your service is at 80% capacity, add another.

  • The 80% rule: When any space or service reaches 80% capacity, growth stalls
  • Launch new small groups quarterly, not annually
  • Consider adding a second service before you think you need one
  • Create entry points for new people throughout the year

4 Strengthen Your Digital Presence

In today's world, your online presence is often the first impression people have of your church. Make it count.

  • Professional, mobile-friendly website with clear service times and location
  • Active social media with authentic, engaging content
  • Online giving and registration capabilities
  • Sermon recordings or livestream options
  • Google Business profile with updated information and photos

5 Cast Vision Relentlessly

People don't rally around maintenance — they rally around mission. If your church has lost its sense of urgency and direction, growth will stall.

  • Revisit and refresh your vision annually
  • Share stories of life change regularly
  • Set faith-stretching goals that require God's intervention
  • Communicate the vision through every channel, every week
  • Celebrate progress and milestones along the way
Vision Rule: Vision leaks. You need to cast it at least once a month through sermons, emails, social media, and personal conversations.

Next Steps

Breaking growth barriers requires intentionality, courage, and a willingness to change. Start with one step, implement it fully, then move to the next. Remember: growth is not the goal — health is. Healthy things grow naturally.
