Financial Health for Churches

A Guide to Budgeting, Stewardship, and Financial Sustainability

By Erskine J Currie

The Biblical Foundation of Church Finances

Financial stewardship is a spiritual discipline. How a church handles its finances reflects its values, priorities, and trust in God. This guide provides practical frameworks for building financial health while maintaining spiritual integrity.

Building a Church Budget

The 70-20-10 Framework

CategoryPercentageIncludes
Ministry Operations70%Staff salaries, facilities, utilities, insurance, ministry programs
Growth & Outreach20%Marketing, events, missions, community outreach, new initiatives
Savings & Reserve10%Emergency fund, capital improvements, debt reduction

Budget Best Practices

  • Base your budget on 90% of projected giving (conservative approach)
  • Review actual vs. budget monthly with your finance team
  • Maintain 3-6 months of operating expenses in reserves
  • Separate capital expenses from operating budget
  • Include a line item for staff development and training

Cultivating a Culture of Generosity

Generous churches are led by generous leaders. Here's how to build a giving culture without being manipulative or guilt-driven:

  1. Model Generosity: Leaders should be the first and most generous givers
  2. Teach Regularly: Dedicate at least one sermon series per year to stewardship
  3. Share Impact Stories: Show people where their money goes and the lives it changes
  4. Make Giving Easy: Offer online, text, and app-based giving options
  5. Thank Donors: Send personalized thank-you notes for first-time and significant gifts
Key Insight: People don't give to budgets — they give to vision. Always connect financial needs to ministry impact.

Financial Transparency and Accountability

Essential Practices

  • Annual independent financial review or audit
  • Monthly financial reports to the board/elders
  • Quarterly financial updates to the congregation
  • Dual signature requirements for expenditures over a set threshold
  • Clear expense reimbursement policies
  • Separation of duties (no single person controls all financial functions)

Managing Debt Wisely

Not all debt is bad, but unmanaged debt can cripple a church's ministry. Follow these guidelines:

  • Total debt service should not exceed 25% of annual giving
  • Have a clear debt reduction plan with milestones
  • Avoid borrowing for operating expenses
  • Consider capital campaigns for major projects instead of loans
  • Refinance when rates are favorable

Planning for Financial Sustainability

Long-term financial health requires strategic thinking beyond the annual budget:

  • Diversify income streams (facility rentals, bookstore, events)
  • Build an endowment fund for long-term stability
  • Invest in systems that reduce costs (energy efficiency, technology)
  • Develop a succession plan that includes financial transition
  • Review insurance coverage annually
